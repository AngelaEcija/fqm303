Ángela Écija Arenas was born in Cabra in 1991. Ángela studied Chemistry at University of Córdoba (Spain) (2009-2014). Since 2012, she has been a collaborator student in the FQM-303 group.

In 2015, she finished her master´s degree in “Máster Universitario en Química por la Universidad de Almería Universidad de Cádiz, Universidad de Córdoba Universidad de Huelva, Universidad de Jaén y la Universidad de Málaga” at University of Córdoba. Her master project was focused in the separation of hybrid nanomaterials (nanoparticles and liposomes) by using sucrose/ficoll/percoll density gradient centrifugation system.

Nowadays, she is doing her PhD studies in Fine Chemistry and Nanochemistry, her research is focused on the development of new analytical platforms for the agro-food safety evaluation using different hybrid nanomaterials (nanoparticles and liposomes). Additionally, she has participated as speaker in numerous scientific divulgation activities such as "European Researchers’ Night", "Café con Ciencia", "Jornadas de Introducción al Laboratorio" or diffusion of the reseach lines of the group in different meetings.

Vanesa Román Pizarro was born in Talavera de la Reina in 1987. Vanesa studied Chemistry at University of Córdoba (2005-2010). Since 2010, she has been a collaborator student in the FQM-303 group. In 2011, she finished a Master in Fine Advanced Chemistry. Her master project was focused in the synthesis and characterization of Gold Nanoparticles-Liposome Hybrids and their application to determine biotin in food samples.

In 2014, she received a predoctoral grant (FPI) from Ministry of Economia and Competitividad to do her PhD. Later, in 2016, she did a predoctoral stay at the University of Regensburg (Germany) for three months. There, she could synthetize a new kind of hybrid liposomes to determine Laccase inhibitors in beverages.

Nowadays, she is doing her PhD studies in Fine Chemistry and Nanochemistry, her research is focused on the development of new analytical method on a microfluid scale, using different hybrid nanomaterials (nanoparticles and liposomes) such as analytical tools. Additionally, she has participated as speaker in numerous scientific divulgation activities such as "European Researchers’ Night", "Café con Ciencia", "Jornadas de Introducción al Laboratorio" or diffusion of the reseach lines of the group in different meetings.

Mª del Valle Zurita Lozano was born in Córdoba in 1994. Valle studied Biochemistry at University of Córdoba (2012-2016); in 2013, she joined the FQM-303 group as collaborator student. In 2015, she obtained an official research scholarship from Ministry of Education, Culture and Sport called “Beca de Colaboración” to develop a lateral flow biosensor to determine soy in milk. In 2016, she obtained another scholarship from University of Córdoba named “Beca de Iniciación a la Investigación” to develop an analytical method to determine allergen proteins in milk. In 2018, she finished her master’s in advanced chemistry at University of Córdoba and she obtained extraordinary prize.

Nowadays, she is doing her PhD studies in Fine Chemistry and Nanochemistry. Her research is focused on extractive and (bio)sensor analytical platforms, where she uses nanomaterials, liquid chromatography with DAD and fluorometer, ultraviolet spectroscopy and fluorescence as separation and detection techniques.

For the diffusion of the science, she has contributed participating in different events of the University of Córdoba: "Paseo por la Ciencia", "Noche Europea de los Investigadores", "Jornadas de Introducción al Laboratorio" or diffusion of the reseach lines of the group in different meetings.
